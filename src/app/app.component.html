About

Pri Mi

My name is Mia Coupland and I am a software developer based in Newcastle Upon-Tyne. I am currently undertaking a Masters in Computer Science at Newcastle University and have a background in history. I am a cultural historian who studied my undergraduate degree at the University of Manchester and I am interested in merging the two fields of history and computer science together. I would like to use development for humanitarian or accessibility purposes.

I have specialised in the history of China's one-child policy and the effects of international adoption, however I have always wanted to pursue software development and only recently received the opportunity. I am hoping to use my existing knowledge in this field to build my final dissertation project - studying the creation of a database to help facilitate family re-unification.

I have a wide range of experience from fraud prevention, to captioning/respeaking, to IT. My current role is in developing P&G's internal Time and WorkForce management system. I am the Time & Attendance specialist for Europe and utilise JavaScript, Crystal Reporting, and MySQL skills. My daily role includes implementation, analysis, and testing of the system to accommodate for any incidents the plants come across, or any requested changes. I monitor the payroll and timesheets and the system ensures that these are accurately calculated and tailored to each individual region/country's local laws, union requirements, etc.
